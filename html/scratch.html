About the Blue Jay

The Blue Jay (Cyanocitta cristata) is one of North America's most recognizable birds. Known for their striking blue, white, and black plumage, Blue Jays are intelligent, social, and surprisingly complex creatures. They are members of the Corvidae family, making them relatives of crows and ravens.

Blue Jay Facts

  1. Appearance
    • Vibrant blue feathers on wings and tail
    • White and black markings on the face and wings
    • Distinctive blue crest on top of the head
  2. Behavior
    • Highly intelligent and known to use tools
    • Mimic the calls of hawks to scare away other birds
    • Mate for life and are very loyal partners
  3. Diet
    • Acorns are their favorite food
    • Also eat insects, seeds, and berries
    • Known to store food for winter
